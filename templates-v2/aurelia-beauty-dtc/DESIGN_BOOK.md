# Aurelia Beauty DTC - Design Book

## Project Name
Aurelia Beauty DTC

## Product Type
Premium direct-to-consumer skincare commerce experience.

## Intended Audience
Beauty founders, skincare retailers, DTC teams, subscription commerce operators, and agencies building premium product-led storefronts.

## User Problem
Skincare customers are overwhelmed by overlapping products, vague ingredient claims, and routine complexity. Aurelia helps shoppers choose by skin goal, understand ingredient purpose, and buy compact bundles with refill logic.

## UX Concept
The experience sells through education first: product-led hero, ingredient cards, accessible before/after comparison, guided routine builder, skin-goal bundles, verified reviews, and a transparent subscription CTA.

## Layout Structure
Sticky top navigation, two-column product hero, proof metrics, ingredient grid, before/after panel, desktop routine builder with sticky quiz, product bundle cards, review band, subscription form, and utility footer.

## Typography System
Cormorant Garamond handles elegant headings and product storytelling. DM Sans handles interface labels, body copy, buttons, forms, and commerce controls.

## Color System
Cream `#FFF8F0`, rose quartz `#F7C7D9`, terracotta `#C76F56`, cocoa `#5B3A32`, pearl `#FDF2F8`, and sage `#A8BFA0`.

## Component System
Core components include nav drawer, hero product stage, proof cards, ingredient cards, comparison cards, quiz goal buttons, routine step cards, bundle cards, review cards, and subscription form.

## Interaction Rules
Mobile drawer updates `aria-expanded` and `aria-hidden`. Quiz options use single-selected state. Primary actions are direct routine or bundle paths.

## Responsive Behavior
Desktop emphasizes product theatre plus guided commerce. Tablet collapses grids to two columns. Mobile turns the routine builder into a linear guided quiz with a visible progress bar and stacked commerce cards.

## Accessibility Notes
Semantic landmarks, skip link, visible focus states, labeled controls, non-color-only status text, readable contrast, accessible comparison copy, and reduced-motion fallback are included.

## Performance Notes
The skeleton is a standalone HTML file with inline CSS and minimal JavaScript. Visual product assets are CSS-built product forms to avoid external image weight.

## Commercial Use Cases
DTC skincare launch, premium beauty storefront, routine quiz prototype, subscription refill funnel, ingredient education page, and campaign-specific product bundle landing page.
