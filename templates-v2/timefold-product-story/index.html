Past / origin story

It began with launch teams rebuilding context every Monday.

Before Timefold, product launches were run through disconnected documents. The founding team interviewed product leaders at healthcare, fintech, and developer-tool companies and found the same failure mode: people could see tasks, but not the reasoning that shaped the release.

Customer signalBeta teams wanted a record of why scope changed, not just what changed.
Design principleThe roadmap should behave like an accountable product journal.
First winA pilot reduced launch-status meetings from four per week to one.
Original pilot6 teams

Past / version 1

The first release room made decisions visible.

Version 1 connected release milestones, customer quotes, launch assets, and dependency notes. It was deliberately narrow: one product version, one accountable story, one source of truth for every team carrying the release into market.

Scope ledgerEvery dropped feature kept its reason, owner, and follow-up date.
Go-to-market mapMarketing and support saw changes before enablement material drifted.
Launch pulseReadiness moved from slide updates to live operational health.
Status calls removed38%

Present / product now

Timefold is now the shared memory for product momentum.

Modern Timefold gives product, engineering, sales, marketing, and customer teams a simultaneous view of what shipped, what changed today, and what future releases depend on. The present is always reachable because it is where decisions get made.
